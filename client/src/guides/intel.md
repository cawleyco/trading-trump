Intel is the aggregate layer over the Congress-trade archive: six read-only dashboards that answer "what is Congress doing lately" from different angles. Everything recomputes on each load.

## How to use each tab

- **Most active** — the most bought/sold tickers over the last 30 days, with net sentiment (buys − sells) and average copy score. Start here for "what's hot".
- **Sector heatmap** — sector × week grid of net buys over 90 days. Diverging colors: green = net buying, red = net selling.
- **Committee heatmap** — committee × sector trade counts over 180 days, via committee-membership joins. Shows *which committees' members* trade *which sectors*.
- **Exposed stocks** — stocks ranked by a composite conflict-risk index blending Congress trades, lobbying filings, and federal contracts. High = "positioned to know something", never "proof of anything".
- **Disclosure quality** — the filing-speed leaderboard: per-politician median disclosure lag and the share filed within 15/30/45 days. Fast filers make fresher, more copyable signals.
- **Copy performance** — disclosure-basis backtest returns vs SPY, plus a per-strategy match summary. The honest "did copying actually work" tab.

## How to add something to your watchlist

Rows with a **Watch** button (tickers, politicians) can be added to the watchlist directly. Watched items surface in the Overview watchlist panel and can trigger `watchlist-activity` alert rules — see the Alerts guide.

## Tips & caveats

- These are historical aggregates, not predictions. They organize evidence; they do not manufacture edge.
- A quiet cell can mean "no data", not "no activity": trades whose ticker didn't resolve to a sector, or whose politician didn't link to a committee, are silently absent from the grids.
- Conflict-risk is association, not causation. Treat a high index as "worth a closer look", not an accusation.
